## Post 1: How to Run a Calm Marketing Org (Like a Product Team)

**Core idea:** Run your marketing team with the same operational discipline as a product/engineering team. 

### 1. Stop saying yes to everything and build a backlog
A backlog is simply a prioritized list of work. Every campaign, project, and random sales request lives in one place. When the head of sales asks for a 1-pager by Friday, the answer isn't yes or no. It's: "We will put it in the backlog and prioritize it against everything else we have going on."

### 2. Plan in 2-week sprints, not annual marketing plans
The annual marketing plan is mostly a fiction. By February, competitors launch new features or priorities shift, and half the plan is irrelevant. You still need a 1-page strategy (targets, revenue goals, major plays), but actual work should be planned in two-week blocks. Do quick daily standups and sprint reviews where the team presents what they shipped.

### 3. Start finishing, stop starting
Most marketing teams have 10+ open projects at any time. Everyone is busy, but nothing ships. Start finishing things before you start new ones. Focus on the smallest viable thing you can ship right now to get market feedback, instead of waiting for a massive launch.
