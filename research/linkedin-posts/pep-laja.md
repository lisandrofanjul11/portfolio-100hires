## Post 1: The Competitor Copycat Framework

**Core idea:** When a competitor copies your messaging or positioning, your reaction should be dictated entirely by *their* market size, not your ego. 

### The Reaction Matrix
*   **Scenario A: A Category Leader (Top 3) copies you.** 
    *   *Action:* **Pivot immediately.**
    *   *Why:* They have the budget, brand recognition, and reach to out-shout you using your own message. You cannot win a volume war against them. Find a sharper, new angle.
*   **Scenario B: A smaller player (outside Top 5) copies you.**
    *   *Action:* **Do nothing. Ignore them.**
    *   *Why:* Buyers shortlist the biggest brands, not the closest feature matches. Nobody knows the smaller player exists, so they pose no threat to your market share.

### The Golden Rule of Competitive Panic
Your real competitor is always the most well-known name in the space, even if a smaller player overlaps your product 100%. Don't shift your positioning just because a bottom-tier competitor lifts your copy. Double down on your own differentiation.

## Post 2: B2B SaaS Pricing Strategy & Blindspots

**Core idea:** Common pricing mistakes in B2B SaaS compound over time. How you display and determine your pricing directly impacts your ability to optimize revenue.

### Key Pricing Rules & Traps to Avoid
*   **The Inertia Trap:** If you haven't changed your pricing in 3+ years, you are overwhelmingly likely to be underpricing. Inertia has a compounding cost.
*   **The Competitor-Anchoring Trap:** Basing your pricing on competitor benchmarks directly leads to a "race to the bottom" and a failure to capture your product's actual value.
*   **Gated Pricing Kills CRO:** Hiding your pricing (common in Enterprise sales) means you cannot A/B test it. The pricing page is the highest-leverage Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) target in SaaS. If you don't publish prices, you can't test them.
*   **The Data Disconnect:** Leaders often complain about not knowing their customers' "willingness to pay" (WTP) or price elasticity, yet refuse to run actual WTP or conjoint studies. You have to actively collect the data you wish you had.
