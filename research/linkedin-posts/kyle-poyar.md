## Post 1: The 4-Layer AI GTM (Go-To-Market) System

**Core idea:** Most marketing teams use AI like a treadmill—constantly re-explaining context. To scale AI, you need to build a 4-layer architecture where context is permanently stored, and workflows are automated. 

### The 4 Layers of an AI Marketing Workspace
1. **System of Context:** The foundation. Use markdown (`.md`) files loaded into your AI workspace (like Claude) that permanently define your role, industry, company positioning, and previous work.
2. **System of Skills:** The execution layer. Turn that static context into specific GTM actions with predefined prompts/skills (e.g., "Draft LinkedIn content," "Write ad copy," "Create outbound sequences").
3. **System of Orchestration:** The logic layer. Set up agents and hooks that enforce your brand rules and decide which skills to run, when, and on what inputs.
4. **System of Integrations:** The routing layer. Connect the AI to external tools so it can automatically pull fresh data (inputs) and push final content to live destinations (outputs).

**The Ultimate Goal:** When you onboard a new marketing hire or freelancer, you don't just hand them a brand book—you hand them access to this pre-loaded AI GTM system with all the context and skills ready to go.

## Post 2: The "Animal" SaaS GTM Framework (Target Customer & Pricing)

**Core idea:** Your initial pricing and target customer (the "animal" you hunt) dictate your long-term SaaS trajectory, churn rates, and growth speed. Startups become what they hunt, and changing animals later is extremely difficult.

### The 4 Target Profiles (Based on Christoph Janz's Framework)
*   🐭 **Hunting Mice:** 1 million customers paying $100/year.
*   🐰 **Hunting Rabbits:** 100,000 customers paying $1,000/year ($100/mo).
*   🦌 **Hunting Deer:** 10,000 customers paying $10,000/year.
*   🐘 **Hunting Elephants:** 1,000 customers paying $100,000/year.

### Key Data Insights for GTM Strategy
*   **The Sweet Spot for Traction:** Hunting Rabbits ($100/mo) is the most popular path to $10k MRR. It's low enough for early adopters but high enough to get serious product feedback.
*   **The Churn Reality:** Churn correlates inversely with price. Mice and Rabbit hunters face brutal early churn (GRR of 24-31%). Deer hunters see ~70% retention, and Elephant hunters hit near 100%. Don't panic over high churn if you are hunting mice—it's built into the model.
*   **The Inertia Rule:** 70% of companies keep the same target customer 3+ years later. If you want to move upmarket later, the most common successful shift is moving just one tier up (e.g., from Rabbits to Deer).
*   **The Growth Winner:** In the long run (3+ years), Deer hunters ($10k/yr) grow 3-5x faster than the other groups.
