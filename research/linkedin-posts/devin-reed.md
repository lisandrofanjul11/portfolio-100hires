## Post 1: The Conversational Copywriting Framework for B2B

**Core idea:** B2B buyers are just consumers at work. They don't want to read dense, academic whitepapers or jargon-filled corporate manifestos. To win, you must write conversational copy that sounds like a smart peer talking to them over coffee.

### The 3 Rules of Conversational B2B Copy
1. **The "Read It Out Loud" Test:** If you wouldn't say the sentence to a friend at a bar, don't write it in your marketing copy. Reading your drafts out loud instantly highlights awkward phrasing and unnatural corporate speak.
2. **One Thought Per Sentence:** B2B writers often try to cram the problem, the solution, and the feature into one paragraph-long sentence. Break it up. Short sentences create momentum and keep the reader hooked down the page.
3. **Kill the "Franken-Words":** Eradicate industry jargon that means nothing ("synergy," "optimize," "next-generation," "best-in-class"). Replace them with concrete, simple verbs that describe the actual outcome for the user.

### The Litmus Test
Your goal is not to sound like the smartest company in the room; your goal is to be the easiest company to understand. Clarity always beats cleverness in B2B SaaS.

## Post 1: The B2B Company LinkedIn Page Framework

**Core idea:** Buyers actively use your company's LinkedIn page as a silent evaluation tool. It shouldn't be treated as a thought-leadership blog, but rather as a highly optimized extension of your website's product marketing.

### The Data
Based on Dreamdata's analysis of 3.5 million B2B customer journeys, Company Page impressions appear in 15% of SQLs (Sales Qualified Leads) and 7.9% of Closed-Won Deals. Buyers *are* skimming it before they buy.

### The 3 Rules for Company Pages
1. **Message Alignment:** Your LinkedIn "About" and headline must perfectly match your website's hero section. Buyers are heading to your site next; if the messaging is outdated or overly "clever," it creates friction.
2. **Content Segregation:** Stop posting employee thought leadership on the company page. 
   * *Personal Profiles:* Used for thought leadership and industry opinions.
   * *Company Page:* Used strictly for product stories, use cases, feature announcements, and corporate events. 
3. **LLM Optimization (SEO for AI):** Fill out every single field and section on the company page. Large Language Models (LLMs) use LinkedIn as a primary data source. An incomplete profile means AI bots will hallucinate or ignore your company when answering buyer queries.
