## Post 1: Mapping Marketing Strategy to Growth Mechanisms

**Core idea:** A marketing strategy shouldn't start with generic tactics; it must map directly to the company's core growth mechanism. In the early days, prioritize the shortest path to proving value and building internal trust before pitching long-term plays.

### The 3 GTM Motions & Their Priorities
1. **Discovery-Led (Inbound/Search)**
   *   *How it grows:* People search for a problem, and you need to show up.
   *   *Core Content:* SEO, comparison pages, and educational content.
   *   *The Quick Win:* Fix and optimize your highest-intent landing pages.
2. **Product-Led Growth (PLG)**
   *   *How it grows:* Users use the product more frequently or share it to gain more value (activation loops).
   *   *Core Content:* Onboarding sequences, use-case templates, and lifecycle emails.
   *   *The Quick Win:* Improve the specific content that shortens the user's Time-To-Value (TTV).
3. **Sales-Led**
   *   *How it grows:* Reps reach out, build relationships, and navigate long buying cycles.
   *   *Core Content:* Buyer guides, follow-up sequences, and enablement materials.
   *   *The Quick Win:* Build the exact sales asset the sales team has been begging for this month.

### The Golden Rule of Early Marketing
The long-term work (category POV, content engines, event programs) matters, but early on, marketing must earn internal trust. Find your motion, deliver the quick win, and build from there.

## Post 2: Surviving the "Zero-Click" Era

**Core idea:** 68% of Google searches now end without a click. Marketers must stop treating website traffic as the ultimate scoreboard and shift their focus to brand recall and zero-click content.

### The Attribution Trap
For years, traffic was the scoreboard because the click was the only thing we could easily measure. But as AI Overviews, social feeds, and search engines summarize answers directly in the feed, relying on "getting the click" is a losing battle.

### The Zero-Click Playbook
If platforms are filtering and summarizing before a user ever lands on your site, your strategy must change:
*   **Optimize for Brand, Not Just Keywords:** You don't just need to rank for a generic term; you need to become the brand that people (and AI models) look for by name.
*   **Embrace Lower Volume, Higher Quality:** AI-referred visitors will be fewer in number because top-of-funnel questions are answered in the search results. However, the users who *do* click through will have much higher intent.
*   **Change Your Dashboard:** Stop obsessing over pure traffic charts and direct attribution. Start measuring:
    *   **Branded Search Volume:** Are more people searching for your company name?
    *   **Owned Assets:** Is your email list growing?
    *   **Correlation Metrics:** Did overall pipeline/revenue go up when we launched this campaign? (Measuring correlation instead of exact 1:1 attribution).
